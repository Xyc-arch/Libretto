CONCEPT: Descending-step 'sigh' resolution
ID: M-SIGH
FILE: melody.txt
STATUS: core-idiom
ATTEST: 211/314 songs  (GENRE-SPECIFIC: classical 93%, latin_reggae_world 86% … funk_soul_rnb 47%, electronic_dance 29%)
GENRE: pop, soul, ballad, rock, folk
THEORY: appoggiatura / suspension-resolution, 2->1 sigh, phrase cadence
ROLE: phrase-ending, emotional-resolution, hook-tag
FEATURE: descending-step-onto-long-note
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT: A melodic descent of 1–2 semitones that lands on a longer note — the "sigh." The
  upper note is an accented dissonance/appoggiatura that resolves down by step onto a
  stable, longer chord tone, giving phrases a vocal, exhaling close.
EXAMPLE: song_0002 (Simon & Garfunkel — Bridge Over Troubled Water) bar 10 — Part3 F->D# onto a long note [Part3: G#4@1>2 G4@3>1 F4@4>1 Eb4@5>4]
EXAMPLE: song_0007 (Matt Bianco — Half a Minute.1) bar 22 — MELODY E->D# onto a long note
EXAMPLE: song_0001 (Journey — Don't Stop Believin') bar 61 — Part5 B->A onto a long note
COMPOSE: End phrases by approaching a long chord-tone from a step ABOVE (the 9th->root, or
  4th->3rd, or b6->5), placing the upper note on the beat and the resolution longer and
  softer. The dissonant upper note + downward step is the whole effect.

CONCEPT: Repeated motivic cell
ID: M-MOTIF
FILE: melody.txt
STATUS: core-idiom
ATTEST: 184/314 songs
GENRE: pop, rock, soul, folk
THEORY: motivic development, motif repetition/transposition, hook
ROLE: hook, unify-section, memorability
FEATURE: recurring-interval-shape
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT: A short (≈3-interval) melodic shape that recurs many times across the melody —
  the motif/hook. Repetition (often transposed to fit the harmony) is what makes a tune
  memorable and unifies a section.
EXAMPLE: song_0003 (Eddy Grant — Gimme Hope Jo'Anna.1) — Part4 cell [0,-3,3] recurs 23x
EXAMPLE: song_0001 (Journey — Don't Stop Believin') — Part5 cell [12,-2,-10] recurs 12x
EXAMPLE: song_0002 (Simon & Garfunkel — Bridge Over Troubled Water) — Part3 cell [-2,-2,2] recurs 7x
COMPOSE: Invent ONE small interval shape (e.g. up-an-octave then step down; or down-a-3rd
  then up-a-3rd) and reuse it relentlessly — same intervals, transposed in pitch to track
  the chords. Restating the cell 5+ times per section builds the hook; vary the tail to
  avoid literal monotony.

CONCEPT: Leap then step-back (gap-fill)
ID: M-GAPFILL
FILE: melody.txt
STATUS: core-idiom
ATTEST: 184/314 songs  (GENRE-SPECIFIC: jazz 93%, classical 77% … folk_country 48%, electronic_dance 26%)
GENRE: pop, soul, rock, ballad, folk
THEORY: gap-fill (Narmour/Meyer), leap-and-recover, melodic arch
ROLE: melodic-shape, contour, drama
FEATURE: large-leap-answered-by-step
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT: A wide leap (>=7 semitones) is immediately "filled in" by a step in the OPPOSITE
  direction. The ear expects a large skip to be balanced by stepwise recovery — a
  near-universal shaping principle that keeps melodies singable despite big gestures.
EXAMPLE: song_0001 (Journey — Don't Stop Believin') bar 61 — Part5 leap +12 then step -2
EXAMPLE: song_0002 (Simon & Garfunkel — Bridge Over Troubled Water) bar 22 — Part3 leap +9 then -2
EXAMPLE: song_0009 (Led Zeppelin — Stairway To Heaven) bar 128 — vocal leap +7 then -2
COMPOSE: When you want a dramatic upward leap (octave, 6th, 7th) for a chorus peak, follow
  it immediately with a downward step (or two) rather than another leap — the recovery
  makes the leap land and stay singable. Reserve the leap for a phrase climax.

CONCEPT: Predominantly stepwise melodic motion
ID: M-STEP
FILE: melody.txt
STATUS: occasional-color
ATTEST: 109/314 songs
GENRE: folk, pop, ballad, soul
THEORY: conjunct (stepwise) motion, scalar melody
ROLE: singable-melody, smooth-line
FEATURE: mostly-stepwise
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT: Melodies that move mostly by step (<=2 semitones) — conjunct, scalar, easy to sing.
  The corpus's most overtly stepwise tunes are folk/ballad leads, but conjunct motion
  pervades nearly all the melodies between their occasional leaps.
EXAMPLE: song_0009 (Led Zeppelin — Stairway To Heaven) — vocal 79% stepwise [vocal: A4@2>2 B4@4>2 C5@6>5 B4@11>2]
EXAMPLE: song_0006 (Eagles — Hotel California) — Melody 76% stepwise
EXAMPLE: song_0002 (Simon & Garfunkel — Bridge Over Troubled Water) — Part3 66% stepwise
COMPOSE: Build the melody mostly from scale steps (neighbors and passing tones), saving
  leaps for emphasis (see M-GAPFILL). Keep the line within a comfortable range and let it
  walk up/down the scale; this is the safest route to a singable vocal.

CONCEPT: Melodic range & mixed-interval contour
ID: M-RANGE
FILE: melody.txt
STATUS: core-idiom
ATTEST: 250/314 songs  (GENRE-SPECIFIC: classical 100%, jazz 89% … film_score 70%, electronic_dance 44%)
GENRE: classical, jazz, folk, soul (wide); electronic, simple pop (narrow)
THEORY: melodic range/tessitura, contour, step-leap balance
ROLE: melodic interest, expressivity, singability-vs-virtuosity
FEATURE: octave-plus-span + steps-and-leaps
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT: A lead line that spans at least an octave and moves with BOTH steps and leaps — not a
  narrow, static, or purely-stepwise line. Classical/jazz leads range widest (octave-plus,
  varied intervals); electronic leads stay narrowest. The melody axis that most splits by genre.
EXAMPLE: song_0131 (Duke Ellington — Don't Get Around Much Anymore) — Trumpet spans 29 st, steps + leaps  [Trumpet: D6@3>1 ... A5@6>1 ... Bb5@12>1]
EXAMPLE: song_0159 (Dvorak — Symphony No.2, II) — Flute spans 19 st with steps and leaps
EXAMPLE: song_0253 (James Taylor — Carolina in My Mind) — Part7 spans 24 st (e.g. a -9st leap)
COMPOSE: Give the melody room — span ≥1 octave across a phrase, and combine stepwise motion
  (M-STEP) with the occasional expressive leap (M-GAPFILL). For a wide jazz/classical line
  push 1.5–2 octaves with varied intervals; for a hooky pop/electronic line keep it narrower
  and more stepwise. Targets pitch_range / voice_range / interval_entropy / up_ratio.
