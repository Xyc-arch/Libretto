# kb_theory: melody
CONCEPT: Motif
ID: TM-MOTIF
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A motif is a short, memorable musical idea — usually 3 to 5 notes — that acts as
the seed of a melody. It has a distinct pitch shape and rhythm and can be repeated,
moved, or varied. Most tunes you can hum are built from one tiny motif.
CHALLENGE: Play a 4-note idea cleanly and remember its shape so you can recognize it later.

VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: G4@1>2 E4@3>2 C4@5>4 E4@9>8

CONCEPT: Phrase
ID: TM-PHRASE
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A phrase is a complete musical sentence, typically two bars long, with a sense of
beginning and arrival — like a spoken line that ends in a small breath. It usually moves
toward a resting note (often the tonic) at its close.
CHALLENGE: Shape two bars as one breath: keep the line flowing until it lands on the final note.

CONCEPT: Sequence
ID: TM-SEQUENCE
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A sequence restates a motif at a higher or lower pitch level, keeping its shape and
rhythm intact. Repeating the same contour a step or a third higher gives a melody forward
momentum and a sense of climbing.
CHALLENGE: Replay the same finger pattern starting one scale step higher without changing the rhythm.

CONCEPT: Stepwise Motion
ID: TM-STEPWISE
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Stepwise (conjunct) motion moves between adjacent scale degrees — one step up or
down at a time, no skips. It is the smoothest, most singable way to move and forms the
backbone of most melodies.
CHALLENGE: Play an even scale run, each note exactly one step from the last, with steady timing.

CONCEPT: Leap
ID: TM-LEAP
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A leap (disjunct motion) is an interval larger than a step — a skip of a third or
more. Leaps add energy and drama, but a melody usually "recovers" afterward by moving by
step in the opposite direction to stay balanced.
CHALLENGE: Jump a wide interval cleanly, then step back the other way to recover.

CONCEPT: Contour
ID: TM-CONTOUR
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Contour is the overall shape of a melody — the path its pitches trace over time.
An arch contour rises to a high point and then falls back, the most common and natural
melodic shape, like a gentle hill.
CHALLENGE: Trace a clear up-then-down arch, peaking in the middle and returning home.

CONCEPT: Climax
ID: TM-CLIMAX
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The climax is the melodic high point — usually a single highest note the line builds
toward. Most melodies have one climax, placed late, that releases tension as the line then
descends. It gives a phrase a clear focal moment.
CHALLENGE: Build a rising line so the highest note lands as the obvious peak, then ease off.

CONCEPT: Repetition
ID: TM-REPETITION
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Repetition restates an idea exactly the same way, at the same pitch. It is the
simplest unifying device — hearing a motif again makes it memorable and gives the listener
something to hold onto.
CHALLENGE: Play the same short idea twice identically, matching pitch, rhythm, and touch.

CONCEPT: Variation
ID: TM-VARIATION
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Variation restates an idea but changes it slightly — altering a few pitches, the
rhythm, or adding a passing note — while keeping it recognizable. It keeps music fresh
without losing the original identity.

CONCEPT: Call and Response
ID: TM-CALL-RESPONSE
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Call and response is a two-part dialogue: the first phrase "asks" an open question
ending unresolved (often up in the air), and the second "answers" by resolving down to a
stable note. The two bars feel like question and reply.
CHALLENGE: Play an open-ended question in bar 1, then answer it with a settled reply in bar 2.

CONCEPT: Passing Tone
ID: TM-PASSING-TONE
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A passing tone is a non-chord note that fills the gap between two chord tones a third
apart, reached and left by step in the same direction. It is a brief, unstressed dissonance
that smooths the line (e.g. C–D–E where D passes between C and E).
CHALLENGE: Insert a stepwise note between two chord tones and pass through it without leaning on it.

CONCEPT: Neighbor Tone
ID: TM-NEIGHBOR-TONE
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A neighbor tone steps away from a chord tone and then returns to it — up a step and
back (upper neighbor) or down a step and back (lower neighbor). It decorates a held pitch
without changing where the line is going (e.g. E–F–E).
CHALLENGE: Step away from a note by one step and return to the same note, decorating it.

CONCEPT: Appoggiatura
ID: TM-APPOGGIATURA
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: An appoggiatura is an accented non-chord tone that falls on the beat — taking the
stress away from the chord — and then resolves down by step to a chord tone. The dissonance
lands first and "leans" before releasing (e.g. F on the beat over a C chord, resolving to E).
CHALLENGE: Land a non-chord note on the strong beat, then resolve it down by step to a chord tone.

CONCEPT: Suspension
ID: TM-SUSPENSION
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A suspension holds a note from one chord into the next, where it becomes a dissonance,
then resolves down by step to a chord tone. The three stages are preparation (consonant),
suspension (held, now dissonant), and resolution (steps down). Here C is held from a C chord
into a G chord and resolves down to B.
CHALLENGE: Hold a note across a chord change so it clashes, then resolve it down by step.

VOICES: Piano

@2 [G]
  Piano: C4@1>8 B3@9>8
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Resolution
ID: TM-RESOLUTION
CATEGORY: melody
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Resolution is the move from a tense, unstable note to a stable chord tone, releasing
the tension. The classic case is the leading tone (B in C major) pulling up a half step to
the tonic (C), giving a sense of arrival and rest.
CHALLENGE: Move a tension note (the leading tone) up by half step to the tonic and feel it settle.
