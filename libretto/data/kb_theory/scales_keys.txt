# kb_theory: scales, modes & keys

CONCEPT: Major scale
ID: TS-MAJOR-SCALE
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: The major scale is the bright, "happy" default scale. Formula of whole/half steps:
W-W-H-W-W-W-H (intervals 1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8). Shown here in C: C D E F G A B C.
The two half steps (E-F and B-C) give it its stable, resolved sound.
CHALLENGE: Play eight even quarter-notes ascending one octave, keeping steady tempo and tone.

KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 90 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 D4@5>4 E4@9>4 F4@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: G4@1>4 A4@5>4 B4@9>4 C5@13>4

CONCEPT: Natural minor scale
ID: TS-NATURAL-MINOR
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: The natural minor scale sounds sad/dark. Formula: W-H-W-W-H-W-W.
Shown in A: A B C D E F G A. Flatted 3rd, 6th and 7th relative to the major scale
give it its melancholy color. Same notes as C major but starting on A (relative minor).
CHALLENGE: Play eight even quarter-notes ascending; hear the lowered 3rd/6th/7th vs major.

CONCEPT: Harmonic minor scale
ID: TS-HARMONIC-MINOR
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Natural minor with a RAISED 7th degree, creating a leading tone back to the tonic.
In A: A B C D E F G# A. The augmented-2nd gap between b6 (F) and #7 (G#) gives the
exotic, dramatic, "Middle-Eastern" flavor and a strong pull to the tonic.
CHALLENGE: Negotiate the wide augmented-2nd leap (F to G#) smoothly and in time.

CONCEPT: Melodic minor scale (ascending)
ID: TS-MELODIC-MINOR
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Ascending melodic minor RAISES both the 6th and 7th degrees vs natural minor,
smoothing the path to the tonic. In A: A B C D E F# G# A. (Classically the descending
form reverts to natural minor; this entry shows the ascending form only.)
CHALLENGE: Play ascending one octave raising 6 and 7; keep the top tetrachord smooth.

CONCEPT: Major pentatonic scale
ID: TS-MAJOR-PENTATONIC
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A 5-note scale: the major scale with the 4th and 7th removed (1-2-3-5-6).
In C: C D E G A C. No half steps, so it has an open, folk/singalong sound and avoids
dissonance — every note sounds "right" over the home chord.
CHALLENGE: Play the 6 notes (5 + octave) evenly; feel the gaps where 4 and 7 are skipped.

CONCEPT: Blues scale
ID: TS-BLUES-SCALE
CATEGORY: scale

CONCEPT: Chromatic scale
ID: TS-CHROMATIC-SCALE
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: All 12 pitches in order, each a half step apart: C C# D D# E F F# G G# A A# B C.
No tonal hierarchy — every note is equidistant. Useful for runs, ornaments and
modulation. Shown here as 12 eighth-notes plus the octave.
CHALLENGE: Play 12 even half-steps ascending with smooth finger crossings, no bumps.

CONCEPT: Whole-tone scale
ID: TS-WHOLE-TONE
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A 6-note scale built entirely of whole steps: C D E F# G# A# C. Having no half
steps and no perfect 5th, it sounds ambiguous, floating and dreamy (impressionist /
Debussy color). There are only two distinct whole-tone scales.
CHALLENGE: Play six equal whole steps; notice there is no "home" — the sound is suspended.

CONCEPT: Dorian mode
ID: TS-DORIAN
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The 2nd mode of the major scale; a minor mode with a RAISED 6th (1-2-b3-4-5-6-b7).
D Dorian uses only white keys: D E F G A B C D. Minor but brighter/jazzier than natural
minor thanks to the natural 6th (B). Common in folk, jazz and modal rock.
CHALLENGE: Hear the minor 3rd but bright natural 6th; play evenly up one octave.

CONCEPT: Phrygian mode
ID: TS-PHRYGIAN
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The 3rd mode of the major scale; a minor mode with a LOWERED 2nd (1-b2-b3-4-5-b6-b7).

CONCEPT: Lydian mode
ID: TS-LYDIAN
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The 4th mode of the major scale; major with a RAISED 4th (1-2-3-#4-5-6-7).
F Lydian uses only white keys: F G A B C D E F. The sharp 4th (B over F) gives a
bright, floating, "magical/film-score" lift vs the plain major scale.
CHALLENGE: Highlight the raised 4th (B) — the one note that makes it Lydian, not major.

CONCEPT: Locrian mode
ID: TS-LOCRIAN
CATEGORY: scale
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The 7th mode of the major scale; the darkest/most unstable mode
(1-b2-b3-4-b5-b6-b7). B Locrian uses only white keys: B C D E F G A B. The diminished
5th (F over B) means the tonic chord is diminished — it never feels resolved/at rest.
CHALLENGE: Sit with the unstable b5 (F over B); the scale resists feeling "home".

ID: TS-KEY-C-MAJOR

LEVEL: beginner

The most common beginner key because there are no accidentals to read or finger.
Relative minor is A minor.

C-major then A-minor scale pair (same notes, different tonal center).
