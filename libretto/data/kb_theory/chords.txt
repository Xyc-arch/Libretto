# kb_theory: chords
CONCEPT: Major triad
ID: TC-MAJOR-TRIAD
CATEGORY: chord
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A major triad stacks a root, a major third (4 semitones), and a perfect fifth
(7 semitones): C-E-G. The bright, stable, "happy" sound; the default tonic chord.
CHALLENGE: Play the three notes exactly together as one block, then break them into an
even ascending arpeggio with steady quarter-notes.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@2 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 C5@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Roman numerals in a major key (C major)
ID: TC-ROMAN-MAJOR-C
CATEGORY: chord
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Each scale degree carries a diatonic triad. In C major: I=C (major), ii=Dm (minor),
iii=Em (minor), IV=F (major), V=G (major), vi=Am (minor), vii deg=Bdim (diminished).
Uppercase = major, lowercase = minor, deg = diminished. These seven chords build every
diatonic progression in the key.
CHALLENGE: Play all seven diatonic triads in order as block chords and hear the major/
minor/diminished pattern of the major scale.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 8
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@2 [Dm]
  Piano: D4+F4+A4@1>16
@3 [Em]
  Piano: E4+G4+B4@1>16
@4 [F]
  Piano: F4+A4+C5@1>16
@5 [G]
  Piano: G4+B4+D5@1>16
@6 [Am]
  Piano: A4+C5+E5@1>16
@7 [Bdim]
  Piano: B4+D5+F5@1>16
@8 [C]
  Piano: C5+E5+G5@1>16
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Chord inversions (root, 1st, 2nd)
ID: TC-INVERSIONS
CATEGORY: chord
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The same triad can be stacked from different bass notes. Root position C-E-G has C on
the bottom; first inversion (C/E) puts the third E on the bottom: E-G-C; second inversion
(C/G) puts the fifth G on the bottom: G-C-E. The slash names the bass note.
CHALLENGE: Play the three voicings of the C major triad in a row as block chords and hear
the bass note change while the harmony stays the same.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 4
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@2 [C/E]
  Piano: E4+G4+C5@1>16
@3 [C/G]
  Piano: G4+C5+E5@1>16
@4 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Secondary dominant (V7 of V)
ID: TC-SECONDARY-DOM
CATEGORY: chord
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: A dominant 7th borrowed to tonicize a chord other than the tonic. In C major, D7
(D-F#-A-C) is the V7 of G; its F# is chromatic and pulls strongly to G, treating the
dominant G momentarily like a temporary tonic. Here D7 resolves to G.
CHALLENGE: Block D7, then resolve to G; hear the chromatic F# lift the ear toward the
dominant.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [D7]
  Piano: D4+F#4+A4+C5@1>16
@2 [G]
  Piano: G3+B3+D4+G4@1>16
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Neapolitan chord (bII)
ID: TC-NEAPOLITAN
CATEGORY: chord
LEVEL: advanced

(so called the Neapolitan sixth). In C major/minor the root is Db: Db-F-Ab, voiced in first
inversion as F-Ab-Db. It has a dark, dramatic pull down to the dominant V (G).
CHALLENGE: Block the Neapolitan sixth (F-Ab-Db), then resolve to the dominant G; hear the
Db slip down toward the leading tone region.

CONCEPT: Augmented sixth chord (Italian / German)
ID: TC-AUG-SIXTH
CATEGORY: chord
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: A chromatic pre-dominant built on the flattened sixth, with an augmented sixth
interval that expands outward to the dominant. In C, the Italian sixth is Ab-C-F#; the
German sixth adds Eb: Ab-C-Eb-F#. The outer Ab and F# spread to G, resolving to V.
CHALLENGE: Block the Italian sixth, then the German sixth, then resolve outward to the
dominant G; hear the Ab and F# splay apart onto G.
