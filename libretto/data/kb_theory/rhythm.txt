# kb_theory: rhythm
CONCEPT: Whole note
ID: TR-WHOLE-NOTE
CATEGORY: rhythm
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A whole note lasts four beats in 4/4 — the entire bar. On a 16th grid that is a
duration of 16 slots (>16). It is the longest common single-note value; one onset, held to
the barline.
CHALLENGE: Sustain a single pitch for a full bar with steady counting (1-2-3-4) and release exactly on the next downbeat.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>16
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Syncopation
ID: TR-SYNCOPATION

CONCEPT: Short-long-short & the syncopated-cell family
ID: TR-SHORT-LONG-SHORT
CATEGORY: rhythm
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A FAMILY of syncopated figures built on one idea: a longer note that ATTACKS on the off-beat (an "and")
and is HELD across the next beat, flanked by shorter notes. The base is short–LONG–short (0.5–1–0.5 =
eighth-quarter-eighth, `>2 >4 >2`). It scales (sixteenth-eighth-sixteenth `>1 >2 >1`, quarter-half-quarter
`>4 >8 >4`) and COMBINES with variants — e.g. short–LONG–short–short (0.5–1–0.25–0.25, `>2 >4 >1 >1`), or dotted
variants. Use these freely and MIXED; what matters is the off-beat long note tied over the beat, not one rigid
ratio — it is one of the common rhythm patterns a real piece should touch, among many.
CHALLENGE: Attack a longer note on the "and" and hold it over the next beat, flanked by shorter notes — and vary the shape (different scales, or add an extra short note).
