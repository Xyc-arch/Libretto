# kb_theory: rhythm
CONCEPT: Whole note
ID: TR-WHOLE-NOTE
CATEGORY: rhythm
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A whole note lasts four beats in 4/4 — the entire bar. On a 16th grid that is a
duration of 16 slots (>16). It is the longest common single-note value; one onset, held to
the barline.
CHALLENGE: Sustain a single pitch for a full bar with steady counting (1-2-3-4) and release exactly on the next downbeat.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>16
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Dotted note
ID: TR-DOTTED-NOTE
CATEGORY: rhythm

slots (>6); it is commonly followed by an eighth (>2) to complete the two beats. Here the bar
is two dotted-quarter + eighth pairs.
CHALLENGE: Hold the dotted-quarter for one-and-a-half beats, then place the short eighth on the "and" of beat 2.

CONCEPT: Tie
ID: TR-TIE
CATEGORY: rhythm
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A tie joins two note values into one sustained sound. Rather than re-striking, encode
the combined length as a single longer duration. Here a note held across beats 3-4 of bar 1
into beats 1-2 of bar 2 is written as a single half-note in each bar held to the barline; the
sustained tie = one onset spanning the tied value (e.g. a whole-bar hold >16).
CHALLENGE: Hold one key across the barline without re-pressing it — feel the beat pass while the sound continues.

CONCEPT: Syncopation
ID: TR-SYNCOPATION
CATEGORY: rhythm
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Syncopation stresses off-beats — the "and" slots 3, 7, 11, 15 — instead of the strong
beats 1,5,9,13. Onsets land on the upbeats and are held over the following downbeat, pulling
the listener's sense of pulse off-center.
CHALLENGE: Accent the off-beats (the "ands") and hold them over the next beat, resisting the urge to land on the beat.

VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>2 E4@3>4 G4@7>4 E4@11>4 C4@15>2
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Time signature — 4/4 common time
ID: TR-METER-44
CATEGORY: meter
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: 4/4 ("common time"): four quarter-note beats per bar, beat 1 strongest, beat 3 a
secondary stress. On a 16th grid the bar is 16 slots, beats on 1,5,9,13. The default meter for
most pop and practice music.
CHALLENGE: Count a steady "1 2 3 4" per bar, feeling beat 1 as the strongest pulse across two bars.
