# kb_theory: expression (articulation, dynamics, tempo)
CONCEPT: staccato
ID: TE-STACCATO
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Staccato means short, detached notes — each note sounds for less than its full
written value, leaving silence before the next onset. In the note grammar this reads as
short durations (`>1`) with rest gaps between onsets that sit on the beats.
CHALLENGE: Play crisp short notes on each beat, releasing quickly so a clear gap of
silence separates one note from the next.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>1 E4@5>1 G4@9>1 C5@13>1
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: legato
ID: TE-LEGATO
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Legato means smooth, connected playing — each note is held its full value so there
is no gap before the next onset. In the grammar this is full-value durations placed
back-to-back (e.g. quarter notes `>4` whose onsets tile the bar with no rest slots).
CHALLENGE: Connect each note to the next with no audible gap, holding every note to its
full value until the following onset.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 90 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 D4@5>4 E4@9>4 F4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: tenuto
ID: TE-TENUTO
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Tenuto means hold the note for its full notated value, giving it full weight and
length (the opposite of staccato). The grammar realizes it as durations that fill the
whole note value with no shortening — here each beat is a full quarter `>4`.
CHALLENGE: Sustain each note to its complete value with steady weight, no clipping the
end short.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 C4@5>4 C4@9>4 C4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: accent / marcato
ID: TE-ACCENT
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: An accent (>) or marcato (^) marks a note to be played louder/stronger than its
neighbors. The note itself lives in the grammar via its onset placed on the beat; the
accent mark is a render-layer overlay (no loudness field in the core note grammar). Here
the accented notes are the strong onsets on beats 1 and 3.
CHALLENGE: Stress the marked beats (1 and 3) more forcefully than the in-between notes
while keeping the rhythm even.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: slur
ID: TE-SLUR
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A slur is a curved line over two or more DIFFERENT pitches meaning play them
smoothly connected (legato) as one gesture. The slur curve is a render-layer overlay; the
grammar shows the smoothness as full-value durations placed onset-to-onset with no gaps.
CHALLENGE: Play the group of notes under one breath/gesture, connecting each pitch to the
next without re-articulating a gap.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 88 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>2 D4@3>2 E4@5>2 F4@7>2 G4@9>2 A4@11>2 G4@13>2 E4@15>2
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: tie
ID: TE-TIE
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A tie joins two notes of the SAME pitch into one sustained sound lasting their
combined value (e.g. two tied quarters = one half note). In the grammar a tie is encoded
as a single longer duration spanning the tied value — not two onsets.
CHALLENGE: Hold one key down for the full combined length without re-striking, counting
through the tied beats.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>8 E4@9>4 G4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: fermata
ID: TE-FERMATA
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A fermata (pause sign) means hold a note longer than its written value, at the
performer's discretion. The pause symbol is a render-layer overlay; the grammar
approximates the extra length with a much longer held duration — here a whole note `>16`
holding the entire bar.
CHALLENGE: Sustain the held note well beyond a normal count, listening to its decay before
moving on.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 72 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: grace note
ID: TE-GRACE
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A grace note is a quick ornamental note played just before a main note, taking a
tiny slice of time. In the grammar it is a very short onset (`>1`) immediately before the
main note, which then carries the bulk of the value.
CHALLENGE: Flick the ornament note quickly into the main note so the main note still lands
on the beat.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 90 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: D4@1>1 E4@2>3 G4@5>4 E4@9>4 C4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: trill
ID: TE-TRILL
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A trill is a rapid alternation between the written note and the note one step above
it. In the grammar it is encoded as fast `>1` onsets alternating two adjacent pitches.
CHALLENGE: Alternate the two neighboring keys as evenly and quickly as possible, keeping
each note the same short length.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>1 D4@2>1 C4@3>1 D4@4>1 C4@5>1 D4@6>1 C4@7>1 D4@8>1 C4@9>8
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: mordent
ID: TE-MORDENT
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A mordent is a quick three-note ornament: main note, its lower (or upper) neighbor,
then back to the main note, all snapped in before the note settles. The grammar encodes
it as two fast `>1` onsets (main, neighbor) into a sustained main note.
CHALLENGE: Snap the neighbor note in and out cleanly so the main note still feels like it
lands on the beat.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 90 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>1 B3@2>1 C4@3>6 E4@9>4 G4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: turn
ID: TE-TURN
CATEGORY: articulation
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A turn is a four-note ornament that circles a main note: the note above, the main
note, the note below, then the main note again. The grammar encodes it as four stepwise
onsets in that contour.
CHALLENGE: Play the four-note circle smoothly and evenly so the figure flows around the
central pitch.

VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: D4@1>2 C4@3>2 B3@5>2 C4@7>2 E4@9>4 G4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: dynamics (p/mp/mf/f and crescendo)
ID: TE-DYNAMICS
CATEGORY: dynamics
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Dynamics are loudness markings — p (soft), mp, mf, f (loud), and crescendo/
decrescendo (gradually louder/softer). These are NOT representable in the core note
grammar: there is no velocity/loudness field. The grammar below shows only the PASSAGE
and what states; the dynamic levels and the crescendo hairpin are a performance/render-
layer overlay applied on top.
CHALLENGE: Play the same notes while shaping volume — start soft and grow louder across
the line (crescendo) — even though the loudness is not written in the note data.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 84 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 C5@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: tempo terms (Largo to Presto)
ID: TE-TEMPO-TERMS
CATEGORY: tempo
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Tempo terms name a speed in approximate BPM: Largo ~50, Adagio ~66, Andante ~80,
Moderato ~108, Allegro ~132, Vivace ~160, Presto ~184. The same musical phrase can be
played at any of these speeds; the term is realized by the TEMPO header. The block below
shows a 1-bar phrase set at a slow Largo tempo.
CHALLENGE: Hold a steady pulse at the chosen tempo (here a slow Largo), counting evenly so
the speed stays constant from start to finish.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 50 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: tempo changes (accelerando / ritardando / rubato / a tempo)
ID: TE-TEMPO-CHANGE
CATEGORY: tempo
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Accelerando = gradually speed up; ritardando = gradually slow down; rubato = flexible
expressive give-and-take of tempo; a tempo = return to the original speed. These are tempo-
CHANGE overlays applied over time — the core grammar carries one TEMPO header per piece, so
the change itself is a render/performance-layer marking, not in the note data. The two bars
below carry the notes; imagine a ritardando over bar 2 then a tempo at the next phrase.
CHALLENGE: Bend the pulse expressively — gradually slow through the second bar (ritardando)
then snap back to the original speed (a tempo) at the next phrase.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 D4@5>4 E4@9>4 F4@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: G4@1>4 F4@5>4 E4@9>4 D4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
