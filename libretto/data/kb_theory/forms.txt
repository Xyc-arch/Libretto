# kb_theory: forms (musical structure)
CONCEPT: binary form (A–B)
ID: TF-BINARY
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Binary form is a two-part structure, A then B, with distinct material in each
section (often A in the tonic, B contrasting). No return of A. The miniature below labels
each section in the `[chord]`/comment and gives each part its own melodic material.
CHALLENGE: Hear and play two contrasting sections in a row, shaping a clear end of A
before starting the new idea in B.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 4
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: D4@1>4 G4@5>4 B4@9>4 G4@13>4
@3 [F]
  Piano: F4@1>4 A4@5>4 C5@9>4 A4@13>4
@4 [C]
  Piano: G4@1>4 E4@5>4 D4@9>4 C4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: ternary form (A–B–A)
ID: TF-TERNARY
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Ternary form is a three-part structure A–B–A: an opening idea (A), a contrasting
middle (B), then a return of the opening A. The outer A sections share material; B is
distinct. The miniature uses one bar per section with A repeated.
CHALLENGE: Play the same A material at the start and the end, with a clearly different B
in between — and make the return of A feel like a homecoming.

CONCEPT: verse–chorus form
ID: TF-VERSE-CHORUS
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Verse–chorus form alternates a VERSE (changing words/melody, often lower energy)
with a recurring CHORUS (the hook, same each time). The miniature gives the verse its own
two-bar idea and the chorus a distinct, repeating two-bar idea.
CHALLENGE: Contrast a calmer verse against a stronger, memorable chorus, and bring the
chorus back the same way each time.

CONCEPT: 12-bar blues
ID: TF-12BAR-BLUES
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: The 12-bar blues is a 12-measure chord progression built on I, IV and V. In C the
standard layout is: C C C C / F F C C / G F C C (bars 1-4 I, 5-6 IV, 7-8 I, 9 V, 10 IV,
11-12 I). Below each bar is a whole-note block triad: C=C+E+G, F=F+A+C, G=G+B+D.
CHALLENGE: Keep place across all twelve bars, feeling where the IV (bar 5) and V (bar 9)
chords land and resolving home to I.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 12
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@2 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@3 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@4 [C]
  Piano: C4+E4+G4@1>16
@5 [F]
  Piano: F3+A3+C4@1>16
@6 [F]
  Piano: F3+A3+C4@1>16
@7 [C]

@8 [C]

CONCEPT: theme and variations
ID: TF-THEME-VARIATIONS
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Theme and variations states a tune (the theme), then restates it altered —
decorated rhythm, added passing notes, or changed register — while keeping its identity
recognizable. Below: a 2-bar theme, then a 2-bar variation that subdivides the same
contour into eighths with passing notes.
CHALLENGE: Recognize the theme inside its variation — play the plain version, then the
busier decorated version that still traces the same melodic shape.

CONCEPT: rondo form (A–B–A–C–A)
ID: TF-RONDO
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Rondo form alternates a recurring main theme (A, the refrain) with contrasting
episodes: A–B–A–C–A. The A refrain returns identically each time; B and C are distinct
episodes. The miniature uses one bar per section.
CHALLENGE: Bring back the A refrain the same way each time, with two different episodes
(B, C) keeping the listener anticipating the refrain's return.

CONCEPT: strophic form
ID: TF-STROPHIC
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Strophic form repeats the same music for each verse/strophe — the tune is identical
every time (only words change in a song). The miniature states a 2-bar strophe and then
the IDENTICAL 2-bar strophe again.
CHALLENGE: Play the same two-bar music twice exactly the same way, keeping it steady and
consistent across both repetitions.

CONCEPT: AABA song form (32-bar)
ID: TF-AABA
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: AABA is the classic 32-bar song form: the main tune (A) is stated, repeated (A),
then a contrasting bridge (B, often called the "middle eight") takes the harmony
elsewhere, and A returns to close. The sketch below labels sections A (bar 1), A (bar 2,
same tune), B (bar 3, contrasting bridge), A (bar 4, return).
CHALLENGE: State the A tune, repeat it exactly, take a genuine harmonic/melodic detour in
the B bridge, then bring the A tune home unchanged.

CONCEPT: sonata form (sketch)
ID: TF-SONATA
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: Sonata form has three large parts. EXPOSITION states theme 1 in the tonic and theme
2 in the dominant (contrasting key). A DEVELOPMENT fragments/modulates that material.
The RECAPITULATION restates both themes, now both in the tonic. The sketch labels:
theme 1 (bar 1, C tonic), theme 2 (bar 2, G dominant), development (bar 3), recap theme 1
(bar 4, tonic), recap theme 2 (bar 5, now tonic).
CHALLENGE: Hear theme 2 leave home to the dominant in the exposition, then feel it pulled
back into the tonic in the recapitulation — the whole point of the form.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 5
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: G4@1>4 B4@5>4 D5@9>4 B4@13>4
@3 [D]
  Piano: A4@1>2 F4@3>2 D4@5>2 A4@7>2 G4@9>4 B4@13>4
@4 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4

CONCEPT: minuet and trio (A–B–A)
ID: TF-MINUET-TRIO
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Minuet and trio is a compound ternary dance in 3/4: a Minuet (A), a contrasting Trio
(B, traditionally lighter/other-key texture), then the Minuet returns da capo (A). The
sketch labels: Minuet (bar 1), Trio (bar 2, contrasting), Minuet da capo (bar 3, same as
bar 1). Each bar has three quarter-note beats (3/4).
CHALLENGE: Feel the lilting three-beat minuet, step into the gentler trio, then return to
the minuet exactly as first heard (da capo).

CONCEPT: ground bass (passacaglia / chaconne)
ID: TF-GROUND-BASS
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: A ground bass (passacaglia/chaconne) is a short bass pattern (the "ground") repeated
unchanged while ever-varying material unfolds above it. In a single voice the ground and
upper line are stacked with `+` so both sound together. The sketch keeps the SAME 4-note
descending ground (C3 B2 A2 G3) in every bar while the top line varies each time.
CHALLENGE: Hold the repeating ground steady underneath while the melody on top keeps
changing — the fixed bass anchors continuous variation above it.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 84 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 4
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3+E4@1>4 B2+D4@5>4 A2+C4@9>4 G3+B3@13>4
@2 [C]
  Piano: C3+G4@1>4 B2+F4@5>4 A2+E4@9>4 G3+D4@13>4
@3 [C]
  Piano: C3+C5@1>4 B2+A4@5>4 A2+G4@9>4 G3+E4@13>4
@4 [C]
  Piano: C3+E5@1>4 B2+D5@5>4 A2+C5@9>4 G3+G4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: arch form (A–B–C–B–A)
ID: TF-ARCH
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: Arch form is a symmetrical, mirror structure A–B–C–B–A: sections spread out to a
central C and then retrace themselves in reverse, so the piece reflects around its middle.
The sketch labels: A (bar 1), B (bar 2), C (bar 3, the keystone), B (bar 4, same as bar 2),
A (bar 5, same as bar 1).
CHALLENGE: Build outward to the central C, then unwind back through B and A exactly as
they came — hear the palindrome mirroring around the center.

@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4

  Piano: D4@1>4 G4@5>4 B4@9>4 G4@13>4

CONCEPT: pop song sections
ID: TF-POP-SECTIONS
CATEGORY: form
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A common pop layout chains distinct sections: Intro – Verse – Chorus – Bridge –
Chorus. Each has its own character: a short intro sets the key, the verse tells the story,
the chorus is the memorable hook (recurring), the bridge contrasts, then the chorus
returns. The sketch labels: Intro (bar 1), Verse (bar 2), Chorus (bar 3, the hook),
Bridge (bar 4, contrasting), Chorus (bar 5, same hook as bar 3).
CHALLENGE: Give each section its own identity, make the chorus hook stand out and recur
the same way, and contrast the bridge before the final chorus.

  Piano: C4@1>8 E4@9>8

  Piano: E4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4
