# kb_theory: texture
CONCEPT: Monophonic
ID: TX-MONOPHONIC
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Monophonic texture is a single melodic line with no harmony — one note sounding at a
time, no chords. It is the thinnest, clearest texture; the melody stands completely alone.
On one piano voice it is simply a line of separate single-note onsets.
CHALLENGE: Play one unaccompanied line cleanly, never letting two notes sound at once.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 E4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Homophonic
ID: TX-HOMOPHONIC
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Homophonic texture is a single melody supported by chords that move with it — melody
on top, harmony underneath, all in the same rhythm. On one piano voice the melody note sits
on top of a block chord (joined with `+`) so the top pitch leads and the lower notes back it.
CHALLENGE: Sound a melody note as the top of a block chord so it sings above the support.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 84 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3+E3+G3+E4@1>8 C3+E3+G3+G4@9>8
@2 [G]
  Piano: G3+B3+D4+D4@1>8 G3+B3+D4+B4@9>8
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: Polyphonic
ID: TX-POLYPHONIC
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Polyphonic texture weaves two or more independent melodic lines together, each with
its own shape. On a single piano voice this is implied by register separation: a low line and
a high line interleave in alternating onsets so the ear hears two voices in dialogue.
CHALLENGE: Alternate a low-register note and a high-register note so the ear follows two lines.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN

VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3@1>2 E4@3>2 D3@5>2 D4@7>2 E3@9>2 C4@11>2 G3@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: B2@1>2 D4@3>2 D3@5>2 B3@7>2 G2@9>2 G3@11>2 B3@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Unison
ID: TX-UNISON
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Unison means all parts play the same line. When that line is doubled at the octave it
is still heard as one melody, just thicker and stronger. On one piano voice, sound each
melody note together with its octave (joined by `+`) so the two octaves move as one.
CHALLENGE: Play each melody note locked exactly an octave apart so the doubling sounds as one line.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4+C5@1>4 D4+D5@5>4 E4+E5@9>4 G4+G5@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: Octaves
ID: TX-OCTAVES
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Octave doubling reinforces a melody by adding the same note an octave below (or above).
It is a special case of unison used to thicken and strengthen a line. On one piano voice each
melody note is joined to its lower octave with `+`.
CHALLENGE: Double every melody note an octave below, keeping both notes perfectly together.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 92 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: E3+E4@1>4 G3+G4@5>4 C4+C5@9>4 G3+G4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Arpeggio
ID: TX-ARPEGGIO
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: An arpeggio is a chord played one note at a time instead of all together — a "broken
chord" sounded in sequence, usually ascending. The same pitches that would form a block chord
become separate onsets that outline the harmony melodically.
CHALLENGE: Play the notes of one chord one after another, evenly and ascending.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>4 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 C5@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Alberti Bass
ID: TX-ALBERTI-BASS
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: Alberti bass is a broken-chord accompaniment pattern that cycles the chord notes in a
low-high-middle-high order (e.g. C-G-E-G), giving a steady murmuring support under a melody.
On one piano voice it is a stream of even single-note onsets following that pattern.
CHALLENGE: Repeat the low-high-mid-high broken-chord pattern evenly without bumping the rhythm.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3@1>2 G3@3>2 E3@5>2 G3@7>2 C3@9>2 G3@11>2 E3@13>2 G3@15>2
@2 [G]
  Piano: B2@1>2 G3@3>2 D3@5>2 G3@7>2 B2@9>2 G3@11>2 D3@13>2 G3@15>2
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Block Chords
ID: TX-BLOCK-CHORDS
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A block chord sounds all its notes together at the same instant. It is the most direct
way to present harmony — solid, vertical, and rhythmically unified. On one piano voice the
chord pitches are joined with `+` so they share a single onset and duration.
CHALLENGE: Strike all chord notes exactly together so they sound as one solid block.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 80 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3+E3+G3@1>8 C3+E3+G3@9>8
@2 [G]
  Piano: G3+B3+D4@1>8 G3+B3+D4@9>8
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: Broken Chords
ID: TX-BROKEN-CHORDS
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A broken chord plays the notes of a chord one at a time rather than together. Unlike a
strict ascending arpeggio it may move in any order and often repeats notes. On one piano voice
the same chord tones become separate onsets, outlining the harmony in a flowing line.
CHALLENGE: Spread a single chord into separate, even onsets instead of striking it all at once.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 1
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C4@1>2 G4@3>2 E4@5>2 G4@7>2 C4@9>2 E4@11>2 G4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Drone
ID: TX-DRONE
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: A drone is a single low note (or interval) held or repeated continuously while a melody
moves freely above it. The sustained pitch anchors the harmony. On one piano voice the long
bass note is joined with `+` to the first melody note, then the melody continues above its decay.
CHALLENGE: Hold a long low note while playing a moving melody above it without re-striking the bass.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 88 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C2+E4@1>16 G4@5>4 E4@9>4 C4@13>4
@2 [C]
  Piano: C2+D4@1>16 E4@5>4 G4@9>4 C5@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: Walking Bass
ID: TX-WALKING-BASS
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A walking bass is a steady quarter-note bass line that moves by step or chord tones,
one note per beat, giving continuous forward motion under the harmony. On one piano voice the
bass note on each beat is joined with `+` to a sustained chord/melody note held above it.
CHALLENGE: Keep a note on every beat in the bass, moving by step or chord tone under the chords.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C2+E4@1>4 D2@5>4 E2+E4@9>4 F2@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: G2+D4@1>4 A2@5>4 B2+D4@9>4 D3@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
================================================================================
CONCEPT: Ostinato
ID: TX-OSTINATO
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: An ostinato is a short accompaniment figure repeated persistently, unchanged, while the
upper notes move over it. The looping pattern gives a hypnotic, insistent support. On one piano
voice the repeating low figure interleaves with the changing melody notes above it.
CHALLENGE: Repeat one short bass figure identically every bar while the top notes change above it.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 104 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3@1>2 G3@3>2 C3@5>2 G3@7>2 C3@9>2 G3@11>2 C3@13>2 G3@15>2
@2 [C]
  Piano: C3+E5@1>2 G3@3>2 C3@5>2 G3+D5@7>2 C3@9>2 G3@11>2 C3+C5@13>2 G3@15>2
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Pedal Point
ID: TX-PEDAL-POINT
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A pedal point is a single bass pitch sustained or repeated while the harmonies above it
change, creating tension between the fixed low note and the shifting chords. On one piano voice
the same low pedal note recurs on each beat, joined with `+` to the changing chords above it.
CHALLENGE: Hold or repeat one fixed bass pitch while the chords above it change harmony.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 88 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C2+E4+G4@1>8 C2+F4+A4@9>8
@2 [G]
  Piano: C2+D4+G4@1>8 C2+E4+G4@9>8
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Countermelody
ID: TX-COUNTERMELODY
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: intermediate
WHAT: A countermelody is a secondary melodic line that runs against the main melody, giving
the texture two singing lines at once. On one piano voice the main melody note on top is joined
with `+` to a lower countermelody note that moves in its own independent shape beneath it.
CHALLENGE: Sound a lower line with its own melodic shape under each note of the main melody.

@2 [G]

CONCEPT: Call and Response
ID: TX-CALL-RESPONSE
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: beginner
WHAT: Call and response alternates two phrases in dialogue: a low-register call is answered by
a high-register response, trading back and forth. On one piano voice the call sounds as a group
of low onsets, then the response answers as a separate group of high onsets, and the two alternate.
CHALLENGE: Play a low phrase, then answer it with a high phrase, alternating the two registers.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 100 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C3@1>2 E3@3>2 G3@5>4 C5@9>2 G4@11>2 E5@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: G2@1>2 B2@3>2 D3@5>4 D5@9>2 B4@11>2 G5@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Canon
ID: TX-CANON
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: A canon is strict imitation: a leader states a phrase, then a follower enters later with
the exact same phrase while the leader continues, so the tune chases itself. On one piano voice
the follower's delayed entry is stacked with `+` onto the leader's ongoing notes at the offset.
CHALLENGE: Restate the leader's phrase one bar later, stacked over the leader's continuing line.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 96 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C5@1>4 D5@5>4 E5@9>4 C5@13>4
@2 [C]
  Piano: D5+C4@1>4 E5+D4@5>4 F5+E4@9>4 D5+C4@13>4
GRAMMAR-END

CONCEPT: Stride
ID: TX-STRIDE
CATEGORY: texture
LEVEL: advanced
WHAT: Stride is a left-hand pattern that alternates a low bass note on beats 1 and 3 with a
mid-register chord on beats 2 and 4, leaping between the two, under a melody. On one piano voice
the low bass and the mid chords alternate on the beats, with the melody joined with `+` on top.
CHALLENGE: Alternate a low bass on beats 1 and 3 with a mid chord on beats 2 and 4 under a melody.
GRAMMAR-BEGIN
KEY: C major | METER: 4/4 | TEMPO: 108 | GRID: 16th | BARS: 2
VOICES: Piano
@1 [C]
  Piano: C2+E5@1>4 E3+G3@5>4 G2+C5@9>4 E3+G3@13>4
@2 [G]
  Piano: G2+D5@1>4 D3+G3@5>4 D2+B4@9>4 D3+G3@13>4
GRAMMAR-END
